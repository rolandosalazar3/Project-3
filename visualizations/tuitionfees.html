Tuition and Fees


The Cost Breakdown



In the Tuition Fees chart, the top ten universities show Columbia University has the highest tuition fee at $55, 056 while Princeton University has the lowest tuition fee at $45, 320. Columbia University, University of Chicago ($52, 491), University of Pennsylvania ($51, 464), Duke University ($51, 265), and Johns Hopkins University ($50, 410) fall into the upper five universities in tuition fees whereas Yale University ($49, 480), M.I.T. ($48, 452), Stanford University ($47, 940), Harvard University ($47, 074), and Princeton University ($45, 320) fall into the lower five. The calculated average of these universities combined is $49,895. In part, the differences in the costs of tuition can be attributed to items such as campus activities, parking fees, the library, workout and recreational facilities, as well as enrollment and course fees. For reference, the 2022-2023 cost of attendance (COA) for a non-resident at the University of Texas at San Antonio (UTSA) is $26, 352.
